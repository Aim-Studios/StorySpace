StorySpace - FAQs

Which all types of content can I add?

You can submit any form of multimedia content to our issues that have a story-telling value to it. Examples are short stories, stories, poems, comics, animated videos, short films, etc. We encourage the use of English in the contents so we can verify your submission easily, but it ultimately is your choice and you may use any language you prefer. If the content is in a non-english language, we will be using third-party translation tools to verify your subission.

Is there a limit to the amount of content that I can submit?

Yes, to encourage diversity in issues only a maximum of two content per person is allowed in an issue. There are also some limits to the size of the content you can publish. For text-based contents like stories, short-stories, poems, etc. the limit is a maximum of 2500 words per content. For other types of contents like comics, short films, animated videos, etc. the limit is a maximum of 20 MB per file. If your film exceeds the limit, it is advisable to upload it on other social media platforms like youtube and provide us with an external link.

How does StorySpace work?

Once you have submitted you content, your request will be emailed to the site's admin. Our team will then do a quick check and after that you content will be published to the next available issue. If there were any problems with your submission, we will notify you via email. When your content is published, it is uploaded to the admin's google drive from where the website accesses it if needed. We do our best to make this process safe, secure and transparent.

How does StorySpace make profit?

We do not, as of now. The site is an experimental project as is not meant to be commercial or profitable for now. Once the site gains a signifiant number of users, we may introduce ads to make some profit. But we are in no rush. The site will remain free forever, and will never require you to pay for enjoying or publishing content here.

Is the site dynamic?

No, StorySpace is not a dynamic website, meaning it does not make use of server-side programs or databases to provide content. All updates made to the site are done manually by our admins. This includes the reviewing and addition of your contents to the issues.
